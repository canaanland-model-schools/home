What is an Equation: an Equation is A mathematical sentence with an equals sign. Example: x plus 5 equals 12. because there ia an equal sign this mathematical statement is referred to as an equation.

What is an Expression: an Expression is a Math phrase without equals sign. Example: 3x plus 5. because there is no equal sign this mathematical statement is referred to as an expression . next.

What is a Variable: a Variable is The unknown value, usually a letter like x, y, or m.

What is a Solution:a Solution is The value that makes the equation true.

What is the meaning of LHS and RHS: LHS and RHS means Left-hand side and right-hand side of the equals sign.

NOW LETS LOOK AT DIFFERENT CASES OF SOLVING EQUATIONS . CASE 1: ADDITION EQUATIONS which involves (Subtracting the same number)

LETS LOOK AT Example 1: x plus 6 equals 14 then subtract 6 from both sides which means x plus 6 minus 6 = 14 minus 6 on the left hand side of the equation . therefor x = 14-6. then x equals 8. Check: 8 plus 6 equals 14.

LETS LOOK AT Example 2: y plus 9 equals 20 then subtract 9 which then gives y equals 11.

LETS LOOK AT Example 3: m plus 15 equals 30 then subtract 15 which then gives m equals 15.

LETS LOOK AT CASE 2: SUBTRACTION EQUATIONS which involves (Add same number)

Example 1: x minus 5 equals 8 then add 5 to both sides then x equals 13. Check: 13 minus 5 equals 8.

Example 2: y minus 7 equals 12 then add 7 which then gives y equals 19.

Example 3: m minus 10 equals 25 then add 10 then m equals 35.

CASE 3: MULTIPLICATION EQUATIONS (Divide both sides)

Example 1: 4x equals 20 then divide by 4 which then gives x equals 5.

Example 2: 7y equals 42 then divide by 7 which then gives y equals 6.

Example 3: 9m equals 63 then divide by 9 which then gives m equals 7.

CASE 4: DIVISION EQUATIONS (Multiply both sides)

Example 1: x divided by 3 equals 5 then multiply by 3 which then gives x equals 15.

Example 2: y divided by 4 equals 7 then multiply by 4 which then gives y equals 28.

Example 3: m divided by 5 equals 9 then multiply by 5 which then gives m equals 45.

TWO-STEP EQUATIONS (Undo plus or minus first, then multiply or divide)

Example 1: 2x plus 5 equals 13 then subtract 5 then 2x equals 8 then divide by 2 then x equals 4. Check: 2 times 4 plus 5 equals 13.

Example 2: 3y minus 7 equals 14 then add 7 then 3y equals 21 then divide by 3 then y equals 7.

Example 3: 5m plus 8 equals 33 then subtract 8 then 5m equals 25 then divide by 5 then m equals 5.

Example with division: x divided by 2 plus 3 equals 10 then subtract 3 then x divided by 2 equals 7 then multiply by 2 then x equals 14.

VARIABLES ON BOTH SIDES

Example 1: 5x equals 3x plus 10 then subtract 3x then 2x equals 10 then divide by 2 then x equals 5. Check: 5 times 5 equals 25, and 3 times 5 plus 10 equals 25.

Example 2: 7y minus 4 equals 5y plus 6 then subtract 5y then 2y minus 4 equals 6 then add 4 then 2y equals 10 then divide by 2 then y equals 5.

REAL-LIFE WORD PROBLEMS (Step-by-step)

Problem 1: A number increased by 7 gives 20. Let the number be x then x plus 7 equals 20 then subtract 7 then x equals 13. The number is 13.

Problem 2: Three times a number is 42 then 3x equals 42 then divide by 3 then x equals 14. The number is 14.

Problem 3: John is x years old. In 5 years he will be 18 then x plus 5 equals 18 then subtract 5 then x equals 13. John is 13 years old now.

Problem 4: A pen costs N x. Five pens cost N75 then 5x equals 75 then divide by 5 then x equals 15. One pen costs N15.

Problem 5: Mary spent N250, she has N150 left then m minus 250 equals 150 then add 250 then m equals 400. She had N400 at first.

QUICK SUMMARY

An equation has an equals sign. The variable is unknown. Use inverse operations. Keep both sides balanced. Always check your answer. For word problems, let x be the unknown, form the equation, solve, and write your answer in a sentence.
